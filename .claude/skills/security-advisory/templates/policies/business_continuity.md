## Purpose
This policy establishes a comprehensive framework for ensuring {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} can continue critical business operations during and after disruptive events, including natural disasters, cyberattacks, pandemics, infrastructure failures, and other emergencies. The policy provides guidelines for business impact analysis, continuity planning, disaster recovery procedures, and crisis management to minimize operational disruption, protect organizational assets, and ensure rapid recovery of essential services. By implementing effective business continuity and disaster recovery practices, this policy aims to maintain customer service levels, protect revenue streams, preserve organizational reputation, and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements while building organizational resilience against various threat scenarios.

## Scope
This policy applies to all {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and third-party service providers involved in critical business processes and supporting infrastructure. The policy encompasses all organizational operations, information systems, facilities, and services that support business continuity including primary and alternate sites, technology infrastructure, data centers, communication systems, and vendor relationships. This policy covers business continuity planning, disaster recovery procedures, emergency response protocols, crisis communication, and recovery operations across all business units, geographic locations, and operational environments.

## Policy Requirements

### 1. Business Continuity Program

**1.1 Program Governance**
- Comprehensive business continuity program established with executive sponsorship and dedicated resources
- Business Continuity Team with clearly defined roles, responsibilities, and decision-making authority
- Integration with risk management, incident response, and emergency management programs
- Regular program maturity assessments and continuous improvement initiatives

**1.2 Business Impact Analysis (BIA)**
- Systematic analysis of business processes and their criticality to organizational operations
- Identification of critical dependencies including personnel, systems, facilities, and suppliers
- Quantitative and qualitative impact assessment for various disruption scenarios
- Recovery time objectives (RTO) and recovery point objectives (RPO) defined for each critical process

### 2. Business Continuity Planning

**2.1 Continuity Strategy Development**
- Risk-based continuity strategies for identified critical business processes
- Alternative site identification and preparation for various geographic risks
- Technology recovery solutions including hot sites, cold sites, and cloud-based alternatives
- Vendor and supply chain continuity arrangements and agreements

**2.2 Continuity Plan Documentation**
- Comprehensive business continuity plans for each critical business function
- Detailed recovery procedures with step-by-step instructions and decision points
- Contact lists and communication trees for emergency notification
- Resource requirements and logistics for continuity operations

**2.3 Plan Maintenance and Updates**
- Regular review and update of business continuity plans based on organizational changes
- Version control and change management for plan documentation
- Integration of lessons learned from exercises and actual events
- Coordination with third-party continuity plans and dependencies

### 3. Disaster Recovery Procedures

**3.1 Technology Recovery Planning**
- Comprehensive disaster recovery plans for all critical information systems
- Data backup and restoration procedures with defined RTOs and RPOs
- System recovery priorities based on business criticality and dependencies
- Alternative technology infrastructure and cloud-based recovery options

**3.2 Data Protection and Recovery**
- Regular data backup procedures with encryption and secure storage
- Offsite backup storage and geographic distribution for disaster protection
- Backup testing and restoration validation procedures
- Data recovery procedures for various failure scenarios and data corruption events

**3.3 Communication Systems Recovery**
- Redundant communication systems and alternative communication channels
- Emergency notification systems for employees, customers, and stakeholders
- Collaboration platform recovery for remote work capabilities
- Voice and data communication restoration procedures

### 4. Crisis Management and Emergency Response

**4.1 Crisis Management Framework**
- Crisis management team structure with clear leadership and decision-making protocols
- Crisis communication strategies for internal and external stakeholders
- Media relations and public communication procedures during crises
- Coordination with emergency services and government authorities

**4.2 Emergency Response Procedures**
- Immediate response procedures for various emergency scenarios
- Personnel safety and evacuation procedures for all facilities
- Emergency notification and accountability procedures for all personnel
- Coordination with local emergency services and first responders

**4.3 Incident Command Structure**
- Incident command system implementation for large-scale disruptions
- Clear command and control procedures with delegation of authority
- Resource allocation and logistics coordination during emergencies
- Regular communication and status reporting to executive leadership

### 5. Recovery and Restoration

**5.1 Recovery Operations**
- Systematic approach to service and system restoration based on business priorities
- Recovery validation procedures to ensure systems and processes are fully operational
- Performance monitoring during recovery to identify and address issues
- Transition procedures from continuity operations back to normal operations

**5.2 Lessons Learned and Improvement**
- Post-incident review and analysis for all business continuity activations
- Documentation of lessons learned and identification of improvement opportunities
- Plan updates and revisions based on recovery experience and findings
- Knowledge sharing and best practice development across the organization

### 6. Testing and Exercises

**6.1 Testing Program**
- Comprehensive testing program for all business continuity and disaster recovery plans
- Various testing methods including tabletop exercises, functional tests, and full-scale exercises
- Annual testing schedule with different scenarios and complexity levels
- Testing documentation and results analysis for plan validation

**6.2 Exercise Scenarios**
- Realistic scenarios based on identified risks and threat assessments
- Multi-site and cross-functional exercises to test coordination capabilities
- Technology failure scenarios including cyberattacks and infrastructure failures
- Natural disaster and pandemic response scenario testing

### 7. Vendor and Supply Chain Continuity

**7.1 Vendor Continuity Requirements**
- Business continuity requirements included in all critical vendor contracts
- Vendor business continuity plan review and validation procedures
- Regular assessment of vendor continuity capabilities and dependencies
- Alternative vendor identification and contingency arrangements

**7.2 Supply Chain Resilience**
- Supply chain risk assessment and single points of failure identification
- Diversified supplier base and geographic distribution strategies
- Inventory management and strategic stockpiling for critical supplies
- Supply chain monitoring and early warning systems for disruptions

**Facilities and Security Team:**

- Alternative site preparation and logistics coordination

- Coordination with local emergency services and law enforcement
