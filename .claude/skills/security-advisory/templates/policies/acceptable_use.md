## Purpose
This policy establishes acceptable use guidelines for {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}}'s information technology resources, including computers, networks, mobile devices, and digital services. The policy aims to protect {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} from cybersecurity risks, ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements, maintain productivity standards, and preserve the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of organizational data and systems.

## Scope
This policy applies to all {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and third-party users who access organizational IT resources. Coverage includes all devices (owned, leased, or personal), networks, applications, and data services used for business purposes, regardless of location or ownership. This policy governs both on-premises and remote access to {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} systems and data.

## Policy Requirements

### 1. General Use and Ownership Principles

**1.1 Data Ownership**
- All {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} data remains organizational property regardless of storage location or device ownership
- Users must protect proprietary information according to {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}}'s Data Classification Policy
- Immediate reporting required for data theft, loss, or unauthorized disclosure

**1.2 Authorized Access**
- Access limited to business-necessary functions aligned with job responsibilities
- Personal use permitted within reasonable limits as defined by department guidelines
- All system activity subject to monitoring and auditing per {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}}'s Monitoring Policy

**1.3 Device Security Requirements**
- All devices must comply with {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}}'s Security Configuration Standards
- Password-protected lock screens mandatory with maximum 10-minute timeout
- Automatic security updates required unless explicitly exempted
- Anti-malware protection required on all applicable devices

### 2. Security and Data Protection

**2.1 Authentication and Access Control**
- Unique user accounts required; shared accounts prohibited except where specifically approved
- Multi-factor authentication required for systems designated as high-risk
- Account credentials must not be shared, stored insecurely, or disclosed to others
- Immediate notification required for suspected account compromise

**2.2 Data Handling Standards**
- Data classification labels must be respected and appropriate protections applied
- Encryption required for sensitive data in transit and at rest
- Data backup and recovery procedures must be followed for critical information
- Personal and confidential information handling must comply with applicable privacy regulations

**2.3 Network and System Security**
- Only approved software and applications may be installed
- System modifications and configuration changes require IT approval
- Network traffic monitoring and filtering systems must not be circumvented
- Suspicious activity or security incidents must be reported immediately

### 3. Prohibited Activities

**3.1 Strictly Prohibited - No Exceptions**
- **Illegal Activities**: Any activity violating local, state, federal, or international laws
- **Intellectual Property Violations**: Unauthorized use, copying, or distribution of copyrighted materials
- **Malware Introduction**: Installing or executing malicious software, viruses, or ransomware
- **Unauthorized Access**: Accessing data, systems, or accounts beyond authorized scope
- **Security Circumvention**: Bypassing security controls, authentication mechanisms, or monitoring systems

**3.2 Network and System Misuse**
- **Reconnaissance Activities**: Network scanning, penetration testing, or vulnerability probing without authorization
- **Service Disruption**: Denial of service attacks, excessive resource consumption, or system interference
- **Traffic Interception**: Network sniffing, man-in-the-middle attacks, or unauthorized monitoring
- **Account Abuse**: Password cracking, brute force attacks, or credential harvesting
- **System Manipulation**: Unauthorized software installation, configuration changes, or privilege escalation

**3.3 Communication and Content Violations**
- **Harassment**: Sending threatening, harassing, or discriminatory communications
- **Spam and Solicitation**: Mass unsolicited emails, chain letters, or unauthorized advertising
- **Fraudulent Communications**: Impersonation, phishing attempts, or deceptive practices
- **Inappropriate Content**: Accessing, storing, or transmitting offensive, illegal, or harmful material
- **Information Disclosure**: Sharing confidential information with unauthorized parties

### 4. Social Media and External Communications

**4.1 Professional Representation**
- Personal opinions must be clearly distinguished from {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} positions
- {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} branding and logos require approval before use
- Professional conduct standards apply to all online activities
- Confidential information may not be shared on social platforms

**4.2 Content Guidelines**
- {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} reputation and values must be respected
- Discriminatory, harassing, or offensive content prohibited
- Copyright and intellectual property rights must be respected
- Privacy of colleagues and customers must be protected

## Roles and Responsibilities

**All Users:**
- Comply with policy requirements and report violations
- Protect authentication credentials and access privileges
- Report security incidents and suspicious activities
- Participate in required security awareness training

**Department Managers:**
- Establish department-specific usage guidelines
- Monitor compliance within their areas of responsibility
- Approve exceptions within defined parameters
- Ensure staff receive appropriate training

**IT Security Team:**
- Monitor system usage and investigate violations
- Maintain technical security controls and configurations
- Provide guidance on policy interpretation and compliance
- Coordinate incident response and violation reporting

**{{POLICY_OWNER}}:**
- Overall policy administration and enforcement
- Review and update policy requirements
- Coordinate with legal and compliance teams
- Approve major exceptions and sanctions

## Compliance and Monitoring

**Monitoring Activities:**
- System access logs and user activity monitoring
- Network traffic analysis for security and policy violations
- Periodic compliance audits and assessments
- Automated security scanning and alerting

**Performance Metrics:**
- Policy violation incident rates and trending
- Security awareness training completion rates
- System compromise and data loss incidents
- User compliance assessment scores

## Sanctions and Enforcement

**Violation Consequences:**
Policy violations will result in progressive disciplinary action appropriate to the severity and frequency of the offense:

- **Minor Violations**: Mandatory refresher training and documented coaching
- **Moderate Violations**: Written warnings and temporary access restrictions
- **Serious Violations**: Suspension of system privileges and formal disciplinary action
- **Severe Violations**: Employment termination and potential legal prosecution

**Factors Considered:**
- Intent and severity of the violation
- Potential or actual damage to {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}}
- Previous violation history
- Legal and regulatory implications
