## Purpose
This standard defines appropriate and secure uses of Internet services by {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} employees and affiliates while protecting organizational networks, systems, and data from Internet-based threats. The standard establishes guidelines for authorized Internet activities, security requirements for Internet access, and prohibited activities that could compromise organizational security or violate applicable laws and regulations. By implementing comprehensive Internet usage controls, this standard ensures productive business use of Internet resources while maintaining security, protecting organizational reputation, and ensuring compliance with legal and regulatory requirements.

## Scope
This standard applies to all {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, business partners, and vendors who access the Internet through organizational computing or networking resources. The policy covers all Internet access regardless of device ownership, location, or connection method including corporate networks, remote access, mobile devices, and bring-your-own-device (BYOD) scenarios. This standard governs web browsing, email communication, file transfers, social media usage, cloud services, and any other Internet-based activities conducted using organizational resources or affecting organizational security and reputation.

## Standard Requirements

### 1. Authorized Internet Services

**1.1 Business-Approved Internet Services**
- Email services for business communication with appropriate security controls and monitoring
- Web browsing (HTTP/HTTPS) for business research, vendor communication, and approved business activities
- File Transfer Protocol (FTP/SFTP) for secure business file transfers with encryption requirements
- Secure remote access services (VPN, SSH) with multi-factor authentication requirements
- Cloud-based business applications and services with appropriate security assessments

**1.2 Service Authorization and Management**
- Formal approval process for new Internet services and applications
- Regular review and validation of authorized Internet services
- Service decommissioning procedures for discontinued or unauthorized services
- Integration with change management and IT governance processes

**1.3 Access Control and Monitoring**
- Role-based Internet access controls aligned with business responsibilities
- Content filtering and web security gateway implementation
- Real-time monitoring and logging of Internet usage activities
- Bandwidth management and quality of service (QoS) controls

### 2. Internet Security Requirements

**2.1 Web Security Controls**
- Next-generation web security gateway with advanced threat protection
- URL filtering and categorization to block malicious and inappropriate content
- Real-time malware scanning and sandboxing for web content
- Data loss prevention (DLP) controls for web-based communications

**2.2 Email Security**
- Advanced email security gateway with anti-phishing and anti-malware protection
- Email encryption requirements for sensitive business communications
- Email authentication protocols (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) implementation
- User training and simulated phishing programs

**2.3 DNS Security**
- Secure DNS services with threat intelligence and malicious domain blocking
- DNS filtering to prevent access to known malicious websites and services
- DNS over HTTPS (DoH) or DNS over TLS (DoT) implementation where appropriate
- Regular DNS security monitoring and threat hunting activities

### 3. Prohibited Internet Activities

**3.1 Strictly Prohibited Activities**
- Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal content including copyrighted materials
- Visiting websites containing malicious software, pornography, or other inappropriate content
- Unauthorized access to external systems, networks, or data repositories
- Installing unauthorized software or browser extensions from Internet sources
- Circumventing organizational security controls, firewalls, or content filtering systems

**3.2 Security-Related Prohibitions**
- Downloading and executing unknown or untrusted software from Internet sources
- Sharing organizational credentials or sensitive information through Internet services
- Participating in peer-to-peer file sharing networks or torrenting activities
- Using personal cloud storage services for organizational data without approval
- Engaging in cryptocurrency mining or blockchain activities using organizational resources

**3.3 Business Conduct Prohibitions**
- Using Internet services for personal business activities or commercial purposes
- Harassment, discrimination, or inappropriate communication through Internet channels
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential or proprietary organizational information
- Activities that could damage organizational reputation or violate ethical standards
- Excessive personal use that interferes with business productivity or network performance

### 4. Social Media and External Communications

**4.1 Social Media Usage Guidelines**
- Clear distinction between personal opinions and organizational positions in social media posts
- Prohibition of sharing confidential business information on social media platforms
- Professional conduct requirements for social media activities that could be associated with the organization
- Approved social media platforms for business use with appropriate security controls

**4.2 External Communication Security**
- Encryption requirements for sensitive business communications through Internet channels
- Approval processes for public communications, press releases, and external presentations
- Data classification and handling requirements for information shared externally
- Incident reporting procedures for accidental disclosure of sensitive information

### 5. Remote Access and Mobile Internet Usage

**5.1 Remote Access Security**
- Virtual Private Network (VPN) requirements for all remote Internet access to organizational resources
- Multi-factor authentication mandatory for remote access connections
- Endpoint security requirements including antivirus, firewall, and encryption
- Regular security assessments and compliance monitoring for remote access

**5.2 Mobile Device Internet Usage**
- Mobile device management (MDM) controls for organizational mobile devices
- App store restrictions and approved application lists for business mobile devices
- Mobile threat protection and security monitoring for organizational mobile Internet usage
- Data protection and containerization for business data on mobile devices

### 6. Cloud Services and SaaS Applications

**6.1 Cloud Service Usage**
- Formal approval process for cloud-based services and Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) applications
- Security assessment and compliance validation for cloud service providers
- Data protection and privacy requirements for organizational data in cloud services
- Single sign-on (SSO) integration and identity management for approved cloud services

**6.2 Shadow IT Prevention**
- Regular discovery and assessment of unauthorized cloud services and Internet applications
- User education and awareness about risks of unauthorized cloud service usage
- Technical controls to detect and prevent unauthorized cloud service access
- Procedures for legitimizing business-critical shadow IT services through proper approval processes

### 7. Monitoring and Compliance

**7.1 Internet Usage Monitoring**
- Comprehensive monitoring and logging of all Internet usage activities
- Real-time alerting for policy violations and suspicious Internet activities
- User behavior analytics to detect anomalous Internet usage patterns
- Integration with security information and event management (SIEM) systems

**7.2 Compliance Reporting and Analysis**
- Regular reporting on Internet usage compliance and policy violations
- Trend analysis and risk assessment for Internet-based threats and activities
- User training effectiveness measurement and improvement initiatives
- Regulatory compliance monitoring for Internet usage and communications
