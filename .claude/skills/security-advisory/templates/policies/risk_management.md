## Purpose
This policy establishes a comprehensive framework for identifying, assessing, documenting, and communicating cybersecurity and operational risks to key stakeholders within {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}}. The policy provides clear guidelines and procedures for timely and accurate risk reporting, ensuring that decision-makers have necessary information to make informed risk management decisions. By implementing effective risk communication and management practices, this policy enhances transparency, accountability, and risk awareness throughout the organization while enabling proactive risk management, resource prioritization, and strengthening of overall cybersecurity posture. Through standardized risk assessment methodologies, reporting formats, and mitigation strategies, the organization fosters a risk-aware culture and enables risk-based decision-making to protect systems, data, and business operations.

## Scope
This policy applies to all {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and third-party service providers responsible for assessing, managing, and reporting cybersecurity and operational risks. The policy encompasses identifying, analyzing, and reporting potential and existing risks, vulnerabilities, and threats that could impact the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of critical systems, data, and business operations. This policy covers risk assessment methodologies, risk classification systems, risk reporting formats, and communication procedures across all organizational functions, business units, and geographic locations. It establishes requirements for risk governance, stakeholder communication, and integration with business continuity and strategic planning processes.

## Policy Requirements

### 1. Risk Management Framework

**1.1 Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) Platform**
- Comprehensive GRC or similar cybersecurity business intelligence software platform maintained for documenting, tracking, and reporting organizational cybersecurity and operational risks
- Centralized risk repository with standardized risk classification and assessment methodologies
- Integration with enterprise risk management and business continuity programs
- Regular platform updates and capability enhancement based on evolving risk landscape

**1.2 Risk Assessment and Mapping**
- GRC platform maps cybersecurity tools and controls against approved and prioritized safeguards
- Comprehensive risk assessment methodology including threat modeling and vulnerability analysis
- Risk mapping to business processes, assets, and organizational objectives
- Regular risk assessment updates based on changes in threat landscape and business environment

**1.3 Automated Risk Data Integration**
- GRC platform automatically integrates with appropriate cybersecurity technologies to aggregate risk data
- Real-time data collection from security tools, monitoring systems, and threat intelligence sources
- Automated correlation and analysis of risk indicators across multiple data sources
- Data quality validation and verification procedures for automated risk data

### 2. Risk Documentation and Tracking

**2.1 Manual Risk Recording**
- Manual recording procedures for cybersecurity safeguards that cannot be automatically entered into GRC systems
- Standardized risk documentation templates and data collection procedures
- Regular validation and verification of manually entered risk data
- Quality assurance processes for manual risk data entry and maintenance

**2.2 Risk Data Quality Management**
- Quality thresholds defined for each aggregated and quantified risk data point
- Data validation and cleansing procedures for risk information accuracy
- Regular data quality assessments and improvement initiatives
- Escalation procedures for data quality issues and discrepancies

**2.3 Risk Metrics and KPIs**
- Standardized risk metrics and key performance indicators (KPIs) for risk measurement
- Risk scoring methodologies based on likelihood and impact assessments
- Trend analysis and historical risk data comparison capabilities
- Risk tolerance thresholds and escalation criteria definition

### 3. Risk Reporting and Communication

**3.1 Safeguard-Based Risk Reporting**
- Dashboard reporting capabilities for cybersecurity risk on a safeguard basis
- Real-time risk status visibility for implemented security controls and measures
- Risk effectiveness measurement and control gap identification
- Remediation tracking and progress monitoring for identified risk gaps

**3.2 Business Unit Risk Reporting**
- Dashboards reporting cybersecurity risk per business unit and functional area
- Business-specific risk assessments and impact analysis
- Risk aggregation and correlation across different business units
- Business unit risk ownership and accountability assignment

**3.3 Executive and Board Reporting**
- Executive-level risk dashboards with strategic risk insights and trends
- Board-level risk reporting including risk appetite and tolerance alignment
- Regulatory and compliance risk reporting for external stakeholders
- Crisis communication procedures for significant risk events

### 4. Risk Assessment Methodologies

**4.1 Quantitative Risk Assessment**
- Quantitative risk analysis methodologies including financial impact modeling
- Probabilistic risk assessment techniques and Monte Carlo simulation
- Return on investment (ROI) analysis for risk mitigation investments
- Cost-benefit analysis for risk treatment options and security investments

**4.2 Qualitative Risk Assessment**
- Qualitative risk assessment frameworks using standardized risk matrices
- Expert judgment and stakeholder input integration in risk assessments
- Scenario-based risk analysis and threat modeling exercises
- Risk assessment validation through peer review and expert consultation

**4.3 Risk Prioritization and Treatment**
- Risk prioritization methodologies based on business impact and likelihood
- Risk treatment options including risk acceptance, mitigation, transfer, and avoidance
- Risk mitigation planning and implementation tracking
- Residual risk assessment and acceptance procedures

### 5. Stakeholder Communication

**5.1 Internal Risk Communication**
- Regular risk communication to business leaders, department heads, and operational managers
- Risk awareness training and education programs for all organizational personnel
- Cross-functional risk communication and coordination procedures
- Risk escalation protocols and notification procedures

**5.2 External Risk Communication**
- Regulatory risk reporting and compliance communication requirements
- Customer and partner risk communication for shared risks and dependencies
- Vendor and supplier risk communication including contractual risk requirements
- Public disclosure requirements for material risks and security incidents

**5.3 Crisis Risk Communication**
- Crisis communication procedures for significant risk events and security incidents
- Media relations and public communication protocols for risk-related events
- Stakeholder notification procedures and communication channels
- Post-incident communication and lessons learned sharing

### 6. Risk Monitoring and Continuous Improvement

**6.1 Continuous Risk Monitoring**
- Real-time risk monitoring and alerting capabilities
- Automated risk threshold monitoring and exception reporting
- Trend analysis and predictive risk modeling
- Integration with threat intelligence and security monitoring systems

**6.2 Risk Program Effectiveness**
- Regular risk management program effectiveness assessments
- Risk management maturity evaluation and improvement planning
- Benchmarking against industry standards and best practices
- Continuous improvement initiatives based on lessons learned and feedback

### 7. Risk Governance and Oversight

**7.1 Risk Governance Structure**
- Risk management committee with executive sponsorship and cross-functional representation
- Clear roles and responsibilities for risk management across the organization
- Risk management policy and procedure development and maintenance
- Risk appetite and tolerance setting and regular review

**7.2 Risk Culture and Awareness**
- Risk culture development and embedding across organizational functions
- Risk awareness training and education programs for all personnel
- Risk communication and transparency initiatives
- Recognition and incentive programs for effective risk management
