## Purpose
This policy establishes a comprehensive framework for promoting a culture of cybersecurity awareness, knowledge, and responsible behavior throughout {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}}. The policy provides guidelines and procedures for developing, implementing, and continuously improving cybersecurity education and awareness programs that empower employees to recognize, prevent, and respond to potential cyber threats. By prioritizing security education and awareness, this policy enhances the overall security posture of the organization, reduces the likelihood of human error leading to security incidents, and fosters shared responsibility for protecting systems, data, and networks. Through regular training, communication, and engagement initiatives, the organization cultivates a vigilant and resilient workforce equipped to safeguard sensitive information and assets against evolving cybersecurity risks.

## Scope
This policy applies to all {{ORGANIZATION_NAME}} employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, vendors, and stakeholders who access organizational systems, networks, or sensitive information. The policy encompasses planning, developing, implementing, and evaluating cybersecurity education and awareness programs to foster a culture of security awareness and responsible behavior. This includes training sessions, workshops, communication campaigns, simulations, and resources designed to enhance understanding of cybersecurity risks, best practices, policies, and procedures. The policy covers role-based training requirements, general awareness initiatives, specialized technical training, and continuous education programs across all organizational functions and locations.

## Policy Requirements

### 1. Security Education Infrastructure

**1.1 Documentation and Resource Access**
- All workforce members must have access to documentation defining cybersecurity safeguards related to their roles and responsibilities
- Centralized repository of security policies, procedures, and guidelines maintained and accessible to all personnel
- Role-specific security documentation and quick reference materials
- Regular updates and version control for security documentation and resources

**1.2 Learning Management System (LMS)**
- Technology platform maintained for delivering cybersecurity-related education to workforce members
- Comprehensive LMS or similar platform for content delivery, tracking, and reporting
- Multi-modal learning capabilities including online modules, videos, interactive content, and assessments
- Mobile-accessible learning platform for flexible training delivery

**1.3 Training Tracking and Compliance**
- Technology platform for tracking cybersecurity-related education delivered to workforce members
- Automated tracking of training completion, scores, and compliance status
- Individual training records and transcripts for all personnel
- Reporting capabilities for management oversight and compliance validation

### 2. Role-Based Training Requirements

**2.1 General Workforce Training**
- All workforce members receive regular appropriate education on cybersecurity safeguards related to their roles and responsibilities
- Baseline cybersecurity awareness training for all personnel regardless of role
- Department-specific training addressing unique risks and responsibilities
- Regular training updates based on evolving threat landscape and organizational changes

**2.2 Specialized Role Training**
- Engineers, developers, and privileged users receive specialized education appropriate to their technical responsibilities
- System administrators receive advanced security training for infrastructure management
- Business leaders receive executive-level cybersecurity awareness and risk management training
- Third-party personnel receive relevant security training based on their access and responsibilities

**2.3 Cybersecurity Awareness Training**
- Comprehensive cybersecurity awareness training appropriate to individual roles and responsibilities
- Regular awareness updates on emerging threats, attack techniques, and security best practices
- Interactive and engaging training content to maintain attention and improve retention
- Assessment and validation of awareness training effectiveness

### 3. Core Training Topics

**3.1 Authentication Security Education**
- Cybersecurity education program educates workforce members on securely authenticating to information systems
- Multi-factor authentication setup, usage, and best practices training
- Password security, password manager usage, and credential protection training
- Social engineering awareness and authentication-related attack prevention

**3.2 Secure Communication Training**
- Education on securely communicating over untrusted networks including internet and public Wi-Fi
- VPN usage, encrypted communication protocols, and secure email practices
- Remote work security best practices and secure communication tools
- Data protection and privacy considerations in digital communications

**3.3 Threat Recognition and Response**
- Phishing and social engineering attack recognition and response training
- Malware identification and incident reporting procedures
- Physical security awareness and tailgating prevention
- Insider threat awareness and reporting procedures

### 4. Training Delivery Methods

**4.1 Formal Training Programs**
- Structured cybersecurity training courses with defined learning objectives and assessments
- New employee orientation including comprehensive cybersecurity training
- Annual security training requirements with regular updates and refreshers
- Specialized certification programs for technical and security personnel

**4.2 Simulated Training Exercises**
- Phishing simulation campaigns with immediate feedback and learning opportunities
- Tabletop exercises and scenario-based training for incident response
- Social engineering testing and awareness building activities
- Red team exercises and security awareness validation

**4.3 Continuous Learning Initiatives**
- Regular security awareness communications and newsletters
- Lunch-and-learn sessions on current security topics and trends
- Security awareness campaigns and poster programs
- Knowledge sharing sessions and lessons learned presentations

### 5. Training Content Management

**5.1 Content Development and Curation**
- Professional cybersecurity training content development or procurement
- Industry-specific and role-specific training content customization
- Regular content updates based on threat intelligence and emerging risks
- Quality assurance and effectiveness testing for training materials

**5.2 Training Effectiveness Measurement**
- Pre- and post-training assessments to measure knowledge improvement
- Training completion rates and compliance tracking
- Behavior change measurement through security metrics and incident analysis
- Training program effectiveness evaluation and continuous improvement

### 6. Compliance and Reinforcement

**6.1 Training Compliance Requirements**
- Mandatory participation in required cybersecurity training programs
- Training completion deadlines and automated reminders
- Compliance tracking and reporting for management oversight
- Corrective action procedures for training non-compliance

**6.2 Knowledge Reinforcement**
- Regular knowledge checks and micro-learning modules
- Just-in-time training delivered at point of need
- Gamification and incentive programs to encourage participation
- Recognition programs for security awareness champions

### 7. Specialized Training Programs

**7.1 Incident Response Training**
- Incident response team training and certification programs
- Business continuity and crisis management training
- Communication and coordination procedures during security incidents
- Regular incident response drills and exercises

**7.2 Privacy and Compliance Training**
- Data protection and privacy regulation training (GDPR, HIPAA, etc.)
- Compliance requirement training for industry-specific regulations
- Ethics and code of conduct training related to information security
- Legal and regulatory update training
