## COMPONENT 3: Definitions

**Key Terms (Plain Language Definitions):**

**"Content"**
Anything you upload, post, share, or make available on the Service, including text, images, videos, audio, reviews, ratings, comments, and any other materials.

**Example:** If you write a review, post a photo, or upload a video, that's "content."

**"Illegal Content"**
Content that violates EU or national laws, including but not limited to:
- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
- Content promoting terrorism
- Hate speech and incitement to violence
- Copyright infringement
- Sale of illegal goods or services
- Content violating consumer protection laws

**Example:** Selling counterfeit products, sharing pirated software, or posting content that incites violence against a group of people.

**"Content Moderation"**
Our process of reviewing and taking action on content that violates our Terms or EU law. Actions include: removal, restriction, labeling, or suspension of accounts.

**Example:** If you post content that violates our policy, we may remove it and notify you why.

**"Recommender System"**
Automated systems that suggest, rank, organize, or prioritize content shown to you based on factors like your preferences, behavior, or other users' actions.

**Example:** When we show you apps you might like, or rank search results based on relevance, that's our recommender system at work.

**"Profiling"**
Using your personal data to analyze or predict your preferences, behavior, interests, or other characteristics to personalize recommendations.

**Example:** Showing you sports apps because we detected you frequently download sports content.

**"Statement of Reasons"**
A written explanation we provide when we restrict or remove your content, or suspend your account, explaining what happened, why we took action, and how you can appeal.

**Example:** If we remove your content, you'll receive a message explaining which rule was violated and how to appeal if you disagree.

**"Trusted Flagger"**
Organizations that have special expertise in detecting illegal content and have been granted priority review status for their reports.

**Example:** Child protection organizations reporting child abuse material, or intellectual property rights holders reporting copyright violations.

**"Notice and Action Mechanism"**
The process for reporting illegal content or Terms violations, and our procedures for reviewing and acting on those reports.

**Example:** The "Report" button you see on content, and what happens after you click it.
